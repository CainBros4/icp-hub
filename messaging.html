Guide 03 · Message

We don't convince.
We resonate.

The archetype system and persuasion sequence behind how Key Lime lands — three archetypes running simultaneously, distinct psychology for buyers and sellers, converging on a single moment of ownership.

The Frame
"Key Lime doesn't convince people. It resonates with a truth they already half-believe — and gives them permission to act on it."
— The brand's job is not to implant a belief. It's to unlock one.

Buyers already suspect agents are overpriced. Sellers already suspect they got fleeced last time. Resonance creates advocates; manipulation creates churn. The difference shows up in retention and referral numbers 18 months downstream.

Three archetypes, running at once.

The combination is the signature. No single archetype carries the brand, and any one of them alone would fail.

The Combination
Outlaw positioning (the system is broken) delivered with Magician composure (and here's the tool that makes it irrelevant), in an Everyperson tone (and anyone can use it). Operational shorthand: "Confident inevitability."
02 · Audience Psychology

Different problems. Same endpoint.

Buyers carry 80% of brand weight — their archetype stack is Outlaw / Magician / Everyperson. Sellers need more stages of reassurance — their stack adds Caregiver / Sovereign / Mature Outlaw / Concrete Magician. Each audience gets its own four moves and persuasion sequence.

The Revelation Problem

Buyers don't think they're choosing an agent. They think "that's just how you buy a house."

The agent is invisible infrastructure. The job isn't to convince them Key Lime is better — it's to make the agent's role visible, because once visible, it's self-evidently overpriced.

The Four Moves · Buyer
Move 01
Hidden Cost Reveal
Outlaw
Expose what the system is hiding. Tone is curious, not angry — the friend who just noticed something weird.
"The average homebuyer pays $15,000 to someone who unlocks doors."
"In most countries, buyer's agents don't exist. Only in America."
"Your agent's commission came out of your offer. You just didn't see it."
Move 02
Peer Proof
Everyperson
Real customers, documentary-style. Not heroes — normal people their age, in their situation. Neutralizes the fear of being out of their depth.
"I'm 29. I work in marketing. I just bought my first house without an agent."
Move 03
Magician Demonstration
Magician
Show the impossible becoming easy. The Apple Keynote move — no explanation needed, the demonstration is the argument.
30-second video: book a showing, walk in, get AI comps, make an offer.
Move 04
Identity Flip
Deep Layer
Shift who they are, behavior follows. From "homebuyer who needs an agent" to "capable adult who buys their own home." Needing an agent becomes a generational artifact.
"You handled your own portfolio. Your own taxes. You're handling your own home."
"Your parents needed an agent because they couldn't see the listings. You can."

The Risk Problem

Sellers aren't uninformed — they're scared to get this wrong because it's their biggest asset.

The agent, even a bad one, represents someone to blame if things go wrong. That's what agents actually sell: diffused responsibility. Key Lime must provide the same psychological safety without the agent.

The Four Moves · Seller
Move 01
Caregiver Stabilizer
Caregiver
The advisor is the psychological replacement for the agent — not in function, but in the feeling of being held. The seller needs to feel protected, not alone.
"Your advisor is with you through every step."
"Never do this alone."
Move 02
Sovereign Elevation
Sovereign
Reframe: agents don't serve you, they work at you. Key Lime serves you, period. The seller is the ruler of their own asset — not the delegate.
"Your home. Your price. Your terms."
"This is your asset. You decide."
Move 03
Informed Frustration
Mature Outlaw
Not angry — knowing. Validate what they already suspected. You're the friend confirming their instinct, not the activist recruiting them.
"You already knew they weren't earning 3%. Now you don't have to pay it."
"One open house, a listing upload, and $22,000. You did the math."
Move 04
Mechanism Reveal
Concrete Magician
Sellers need the math shown. Long-form case studies, detailed process videos, real numbers. Buyers convert on vibe; sellers need proof of mechanism.
"Here's exactly what happened when Sarah sold her house with Key Lime in 23 days."
Persuasion Sequence · Seller

Validate, protect, prove, empower.

Different from the buyer — sellers need all four stages.

Validate
Mature Outlaw
"You already suspected the agent wasn't worth it. You were right."
Protect
Caregiver
"You won't do this alone. Your advisor is with you."
Prove
Concrete Magician
"Here's exactly how it works. Here's someone like you who did it."
Empower
Sovereign
"You're in control. You decide. You keep the difference."
North Star · The Unifying Moment
The Convergent Endpoint

Buyer and seller diverge in approach,
converge on one feeling at closing: "I did this."

That's the Sovereign archetype reaching its healthy expression — owning the process, not delegating your agency. Everything upstream (Outlaw revelation, Magician demonstration, Caregiver protection) serves that final moment.

The Identity Contract
"I see through unnecessary complexity. I don't pay for things that don't deliver value. I take control of the biggest decisions in my life. And I expect the process to be easy."
