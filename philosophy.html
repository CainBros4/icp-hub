Guide 01 · Foundation

A brand is a bridge.
This is how ours is built.

Why Key Lime exists, who it's for, and the identity contract it offers. Every other decision in the brand — name, color, tone, messaging — gets tested against this.

01 · Core Belief

People don't buy products. They declare identities.

When someone says "I drive a Tesla" or "I use Robinhood," they're not describing a product — they're declaring an identity: I see through the status quo. I don't pay for things that don't earn their price. I choose the future. Our job is to make it effortless for the right person to look at Key Lime and say: "This is mine. This is how I do things."

02 · Mission & Vision

Make the old way feel antiquated.

Uber didn't campaign against taxis — it showed people a button that summoned a car. Tesla didn't argue about gas prices — it built a car that didn't need gas. Key Lime will make real estate agencies obsolete the same way.

Mission

Buying and selling a home, as easy as it should have always been.

Without agents, without unnecessary fees, and without the complexity the industry built to justify itself.

Vision

A world where 6% commission feels as antiquated as hailing a cab in the rain.

The brand must be designed for the end state, not the MVP. Never niche, never FSBO-specific, never anti-agent. It must feel inevitable.

03 · Core Values

Five principles. Every decision tested against them.

These aren't marketing words. They're load-bearing — a decision that violates one of these is the wrong decision, even if it looks right in isolation.

Value 01

Simplicity

The name says it all. Key Lime takes something needlessly complicated and makes it clear. Not dumbed-down — clarified. The process was never inherently complex; the industry made it complex to justify itself.

In practice: every interaction should feel like "Why wasn't it always this way?"
Value 02

Empowerment

Key Lime doesn't do things for you — it equips you to do them yourself, with confidence. The user is never a passenger. They're the driver. The platform is the road.

In practice: the feeling at closing is "I did this."
Value 03

Transparency

No hidden fees, no opaque negotiations, no fine print. Every cost, comp, and document — visible, explainable, yours.

In practice: if it can't be explained in one sentence, redesign it.
Value 04

Fairness

1%, not 6%. Key Lime charges for what it delivers and nothing more. The user keeps the difference.

Never position savings as a "discount" — position it as what the service actually costs when you remove the middleman markup.
Value 05

Intelligence

Smarter than any individual agent — processes more data, negotiates without emotional bias, available 24/7. Never cold. Always in service of the user, never a replacement for human judgment.

AI is personified, not labeled. The user talks to "your advisor," not "our AI."
04 · The Product, Unbundled

The agent bundles five functions. Key Lime unbundles them.

The traditional agent bundles five distinct functions into a single, expensive service. Key Lime delivers each one better, separately, and without the conflict of interest.

The chaperone model is the signature innovation. Key Lime replaces the agent-gated showing model with a gig-economy network — local people who escort buyers through homes for $20 per showing. Like DoorDash for home tours. The chaperone provides access; the AI provides intelligence. This unbundling is what makes the 1% fee possible.

Competitive Posture · Confident Inevitability

Key Lime does not attack agents. Key Lime exists so compellingly that the old way looks silly. Not this: "The 6% commission is a scam — stop funding it." This: "No agents. Easy as pie." Think Lemonade's energy — playful, confident, not combative.

07 · Strategic Guardrails

The fence around every decision.

If a proposed execution violates one of these, it's the wrong execution — regardless of how compelling it looks in isolation.

Must-Haves
  • Never feel niche. The brand works for everyone who buys or sells — not just FSBO-adjacent.
  • Never feel budget. 1% is efficiency, not a discount. Key Lime is the better way, not the cheaper way.
  • Never attack agents. Confident inevitability — the old way becomes irrelevant on its own.
  • Always feel premium. Must meet the standard of Tesla, Lemonade, Warby Parker.
  • Always center the user. The user is the hero. Key Lime is the tool.
Must-Nots
  • Never box into FSBO. Transcend the FSBO starting point — never inherit its reputation.
  • Never lead with technology. AI is the engine, not the brand. Lead with outcomes.
  • Never make homebuying look trivial. "Easy" means clear, guided, controlled — not casual.
  • Never restrict expansion. The brand must work for every market, every buyer type, every scale.
