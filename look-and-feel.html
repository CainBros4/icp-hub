Guide 05 · Inspiration

The creative territory
Key Lime lives in.

Reference brands to sit next to. Campaign archetypes that work. Photography direction. And the competitive visual lane that's wide open.

01 · The Feeling

Not a conference room. Not a luxury showroom.

Key Lime's visual world has one governing image. Every photograph, frame of video, and UI screen should map back to this.

The Governing Image
"A warm Saturday morning, standing in the kitchen of a home you just bought, sunlight coming through the windows, keys on the counter."
A fluorescent-lit corporate office
A staged luxury mansion with no personality
A cold, stock-photo handshake
An agent in a blazer in front of a lockbox
02 · Reference Brands

The brands Key Lime should look and feel like a sibling of.

Each one took a broken, incumbent-dominated industry and made the alternative feel obvious. Study how they did it — the naming, the color, the tone, the product experience.

Lemonade (Insurance)
Primary Reference
Why this is #1: Lemonade is the closest analog to Key Lime in every dimension — name construction (fruit + financial service), visual identity (bright, warm, distinctive), tone (playful confidence), and market position (making an incumbent-dominated industry feel fresh).
NameFruit name that reframes a boring industry — same pattern as Key Lime
ColorHot pink — instantly ownable. Key Lime does the same thing with warm key-lime green.
Tone"Forget everything you know about insurance" — confident, playful, not aggressive
AIPersonified as "Maya" — an AI with a name and personality. Key Lime's future direction for "your advisor."
WebsiteClean, airy, illustration-driven. Directional for Key Lime's marketing site.
Study: lemonade.com landing page structure · Maya's onboarding in-app · the "Forget Everything" launch campaign.
Tesla (Automotive)
Aspiration Reference
Tesla is the "confident inevitability" benchmark. They never argued against gas cars. They built something obviously better and let people draw their own conclusions.
Competitive postureNever mentions competitors. The product IS the argument. Key Lime never attacks agents.
Premium feelEverything considered — packaging, website, showroom, software. Key Lime must feel premium at every touchpoint.
VisualClean, minimal. Lets the product dominate. Lime accents on cream base; let the experience speak.
Feature revealPresented as "of course" — autopilot, OTA updates just exist. AI advisor, $20 showings just exist. Don't oversell.
Study: tesla.com configurator replacing the dealership · launch events — how they present features without comparing to competitors.
Robinhood (Investing)
Accessibility Reference
Robinhood democratized an industry that seemed complex and gated. "Investing for everyone." The same mission Key Lime has for real estate.
Tagline pattern"Investing for everyone" → Key Lime: homebuying for everyone who sees a better way
ColorGreen (financial growth, go-signal) — same psychology. Warmer for Key Lime.
OnboardingGamified, simple, removed every barrier to entry. Key Lime must be frictionless: search → show → buy.
Data vizBold numbers, clean charts, savings made visceral. Savings calculator, comp comparisons, commission charts.
CautionRobinhood was criticized for making finance TOO casual. Key Lime must maintain gravity — this is a home, not a stock trade.
Airbnb (Travel)
Experience Reference
Airbnb turned a peer-to-peer marketplace into an aspirational brand. The chaperone model has direct parallels to Airbnb's host model.
PhotographyDefined their category with photo quality. Made listings feel aspirational. Key Lime listings need the same standard.
Brand warmth"Belong anywhere" — emotional, human, not transactional. Key Lime should feel warm and human, not cold-tech.
Two-sided marketplaceHosts + guests, each with their own experience. Buyers + sellers + chaperones — each needs a tailored experience.
Trust mechanismReviews, verified hosts, insurance. Key Lime: chaperone ratings, advisor track record, licensed brokerage.
Warby Parker (Eyewear)
Value Reference
Warby Parker proved you can be premium AND affordable by cutting out the middleman. Key Lime's value prop is structurally identical.
Value framing"Why pay $700 for glasses?" → $95 premium. Key Lime: "Why pay 6% commission?" → 1% full service.
Premium qualityThe product looks and feels like it costs 3x more. Key Lime must never feel "discount."
Try-before-buyHome try-on program reduced commitment barrier. $20 chaperone showing does the same.
03 · Competitive Visual Audit

Every real estate brand clusters in two zones. Neither is Key Lime's.

The incumbents occupy corporate red/blue. The proptech upstarts occupy clinical purple/teal. Nobody owns warm green — so Key Lime does.

The Open Lane · Warm Key-Lime Green

Nobody is here. The warm key-lime green creates instant differentiation at every touchpoint — recognizable from across a street on a yard sign, distinctive on a home screen among dozens of icons, impossible to confuse with any competitor. Same move Lemonade made with hot pink in insurance.

05 · Photography Direction

Warm. Real. Mid-moment.

Color-grade everything warm — cream whites, not blue whites. Slightly lifted blacks (modern film, not harsh contrast). Green accents in the environment when possible.

✓ Do
  • Natural light, warm golden-hour tones
  • Real people — diverse, authentic, mid-moment
  • Hands with keys (the universal homeownership moment)
  • Lived-in, styled-but-real home interiors
  • The app in natural context — on a couch, at a kitchen table
  • Unpacking boxes, families exploring, FaceTiming from a new room
✕ Don't
  • Harsh studio lighting, flash photography
  • Models, staged poses, stock photography
  • Corporate handshakes, suits, agent headshots
  • Empty staging, mansions, generic luxury
  • Floating screens, tech-render composites
  • "SOLD" signs, agent marketing clichés
The Brief
"If you put Key Lime's homepage next to Lemonade, Tesla, and Airbnb — it should look like it belongs on the same shelf. Warm, modern, premium, and ownable."
— Creative Direction
